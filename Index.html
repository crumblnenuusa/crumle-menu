How Does the Crumbl Cookies Menu Work?

Before you look at any specific cookie, you need to understand how Crumbl structures its menu — because it's not like anything else.

Crumbl runs what they call a Hybrid Menu. Here's how it breaks down:

6 Classic Flavors — Available Every Day, Every Week These are permanent. You can walk into any Crumbl location on any day of the week and these six cookies will be there. No guessing, no checking the app first. They're the foundation of the menu and they never rotate out.

4 Rotating Specialty Flavors — Change Every Monday Every Monday at 8:00 AM, Crumbl drops four new limited-time desserts. These are available from Monday through Saturday. On Sunday, Crumbl is closed at most locations — and when they reopen Monday, the lineup is brand new. If you miss a rotating flavor, it's gone until Crumbl decides to bring it back.

Thin Thursday — Every Thursday Only Every Thursday, Crumbl adds a limited Crumbl Thins option — a thinner, crispier version of a cookie flavor. It's only available on Thursdays, so if you want it, you need to plan your visit specifically for that day.

Non-Cookie Desserts (NCDs) Crumbl has expanded beyond cookies. Some of the rotating weekly slots are now filled by cake cups, cheesecakes, brownies, and other dessert formats. These are labeled NCDs (Non-Cookie Desserts) and count as part of your 4 rotating weekly options.

This structure is the reason people check the menu constantly. The classics give you reliability — the rotating slots give you something new to look forward to every single week.

Crumbl Classic Menu: The 6 Cookies Always Available

These are the six flavors you can order any time, any week, at any Crumbl location in the US. If you're visiting Crumbl for the first time, start here — these are the cookies the brand built its reputation on.

1. Milk Chocolate Chip Cookie / Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chunk

This is the cookie that started everything. When Jason McGowan and Sawyer Hemsley founded Crumbl in 2017, their entire goal was to make the perfect chocolate chip cookie. This is what they came up with.

It's thick, buttery, soft in the center, and loaded with chocolate. Crumbl alternates between a milk chocolate chip version and a semi-sweet chocolate chunk version depending on the week — but both use the same legendary dough. It's served warm, which makes the chocolate melt slightly into the cookie.

Calories: 680–770 cal | Price: $4.99 | Served: Warm

Order this if: You want the classic Crumbl experience without any risk. It's the safest first order and consistently the most popular cookie on the menu.

2. The Original Pink Sugar Cookie

The Pink Sugar is Crumbl's most iconic cookie and the one you've seen all over social media. It's a soft almond-flavored sugar cookie topped with a smooth swirl of signature pink almond frosting. It's chilled — not warm — which gives it a completely different texture from the Chocolate Chip.

It's sweet, light, and almost cake-like in texture. The almond flavor in both the cookie and the frosting is subtle enough that it doesn't overpower anything, but distinctive enough to be unmistakably Crumbl.

Calories: 760 cal | Price: $4.99 | Served: Chilled

Order this if: You like frosted sugar cookies, you're ordering for someone who doesn't want chocolate, or you just want to try the cookie that made Crumbl famous.

Allergen note: Contains almond extract. Avoid if you have a tree nut allergy.

3. Chocolate Crumb Cookie ft. OREO®

A dark chocolate cookie base packed with crushed OREO pieces, topped with creamy cookies-and-cream frosting. It's richer and more intense than the standard Chocolate Chip — the OREO element adds texture and a slight bitterness that balances the sweetness of the frosting.

Calories: 880 cal | Price: $4.99 | Served: Chilled

Order this if: You want something more indulgent than the classic Chocolate Chip and you're an OREO fan.

4. Brownie Batter Cookie

A dense, fudgy chocolate cookie with a brownie-like texture — this one is for people who want deep chocolate flavor without any frosting getting in the way. It's served warm, which makes the center almost molten.

Calories: 690 cal | Price: $4.99 | Served: Warm

Order this if: You prefer unfrosted, chocolate-forward cookies and want something that tastes more like a brownie than a traditional cookie.

5. Snickerdoodle Cookie

A soft, buttery cinnamon sugar cookie — classic and straightforward. No frosting, no drizzle, just the clean warmth of cinnamon and sugar on a perfectly baked dough. It's one of the lighter classics in terms of sweetness, which makes it a good balance against richer flavors in a box.

Calories: 630 cal | Price: $4.99 | Served: Warm

Order this if: You want something simple and familiar, or you need one less-sweet option in a mixed box.

6. Celebration Cake Cookie

A vanilla-based cookie with colorful sprinkles baked in, topped with birthday cake frosting. It's sweet, festive, and designed to taste like birthday cake in cookie form. This is Crumbl's go-to for celebrations — and the one you want in any box that's being given as a gift.

Calories: 810 cal | Price: $4.99 | Served: Chilled

Order this if: You're ordering for a birthday, celebration, or for someone who loves vanilla cake flavors.
